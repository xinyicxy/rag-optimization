**Q1: What must the Engineer obtain before performing research and field work for a task?**  
A1: The Engineer must obtain approval of the Scope of Work from the Director of Public Works/Utilities via letter or email.  
**Text Snippet:** "Upon approval of the Scope of Work by the Director of Public Works/Utilities, perform all necessary research, field, and survey work. Any work performed without approval [...] will be at the expense of the Engineer."  

**Q2: What must the Scope of Work include for a task?**  
A2: The Scope of Work must include a not-to-exceed fee for all proposed services.  
**Text Snippet:** "The Scope of Work shall include a not-to-exceed fee for all services proposed to be provided for the task."  

**Q3: What deliverables are required during the Final Design Phase?**  
A3: The Engineer must prepare final construction plans, specifications, easement plats, contractor documents (e.g., bid forms, bonding requirements), and assist in federal compliance documentation.  
**Text Snippet:** "prepare the final construction plans and specifications, easements plats, and the contractor documents, which shall include bid forms, notice to bidders, [...] federal compliance requirements, and assist in the preparation of other related documents."

A4: The Engineer must consult with and advise the County on the acceptability of subcontractors proposed by the prime contractor.  
**Text Snippet:** "Consult with and advise the County as to the acceptability of subcontractors and other persons and organizations proposed by the prime contractor."  

**Q5: What must the Engineer do during site visits in the Constructing Phase?**  
A5: The Engineer must observe work progress, ensure compliance with plans/specifications, report defects, and disapprove non-conforming work.  
**Text Snippet:** "Make periodic visits [...] to observe [...] the progress and quality of the executed work [...] keep the County informed in writing [...] disapprove or reject work as failing to conform to the final plans and specifications or contract documents."  

**Q6: How many copies of preliminary design documents must the Engineer provide to the County?**  
A6: The Engineer must furnish three (3) copies of the preliminary design documents and cost opinion.  
**Text Snippet:** "Furnish three (3) copies of the preliminary design documents and the Engineer's opinion of probable project cost to the County."
