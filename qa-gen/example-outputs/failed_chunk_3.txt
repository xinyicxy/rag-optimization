**Q1: What responsibilities does the Engineer have regarding contractor payment approvals?**  
**A1:** The Engineer must review contractor payment applications, advise the County on payment amounts, and confirm work quality aligns with project specifications.  
**Text Snippet:** "Based on the Engineer's on-site observations [...] the Engineer shall advise the County as to the amount owing to contractor(s) and indicate whether he approves such amounts; such approvals [...] constitute a representation [...] that the work has progressed [...] and the quality of the work is in accordance with the final plans and specifications."  

**Q2: What is required for determining substantial completion of a project?**  
**A2:** The Engineer must conduct an inspection to verify the project meets final plans, specifications, and contract obligations.  
**Text Snippet:** "Conduct an inspection to determine if the project is substantially complete [...] and if each contractor has fulfilled all of its obligations thereunder so that the Engineer may indicate [...] that final payment should be made."  

**Q3: What format must the Engineer use for submitting as-built plans?**  
**A3:** As-built plans must be provided on blackline mylar film, based on inspection reports and field notes.  
**Text Snippet:** "The Engineer shall furnish a set of as-built project plans [...] on blackline mylar film from information furnished by the project inspector at no additional cost to the County."  

**Q4: Who is responsible for advertising costs related to contractor bids?**  
**A4:** The County covers newspaper advertising costs, while the Engineer pays for distributing plans to Dodge Report, Builder’s Exchange, and The Association of General Contractors.  
**Text Snippet:** "Cost of advertising in newspaper will be paid by the County. [...] The Engineer shall advertise for bids [...] and furnish plans and specifications [...] at the Engineer's sole expense to Dodge Report, Builder's Exchange, and The Association of General Contractors."  

**Q5: What erosion control requirements apply to project designs?**  
**A5:** Designs must comply with County and state erosion control regulations, including an approved erosion control plan.  
**Text Snippet:** "All designs shall conform to the latest County and state erosion control and sedimentation rules [...] An erosion control and sedimentation plan must be approved by the County Erosion and Utility Inspection Department."  

**Q6: What is the Engineer’s responsibility regarding property access for surveys?**  
**A6:** The Engineer must send County-approved letters to property owners requesting permission to enter their land for surveys.  
**Text Snippet:** "A letter will be sent in a form approved by the County by the Engineer to all property owners requesting permission to enter their property for surveys."
