**Q1: What is the deadline for submitting proposals for the Engineering Services RFP?**  
A1: Proposals must be received no later than 2:00 p.m., Friday, October 23, 2020.  
**Text Snippet:** "The submittal [...] will be received no later than 2:00 p.m., Friday, October 23, 2020."  

**Q2: How must proposals be physically submitted to the County of Caroline?**  
A2: Proposals must be placed in a sealed, opaque envelope marked with the RFP number, title, date, and submission time in the lower left-hand corner.  
**Text Snippet:** "Proposals shall be placed in a sealed, opaque envelope, marked in the lower left-hand corner with the R.F.P. number, title, and date, and hour proposals are scheduled to be received."  

**Q3: What is the deadline for submitting technical questions about the RFP?**  
A3: Technical questions must be submitted to Joseph C. Schiebel by 2:00 p.m. on Friday, October 16, 2020.  
**Text Snippet:** "Technical questions [...] should be submitted to Joseph C. Schiebel [...] by no later than 2:00 p.m. on Friday, October 16, 2020."  

**Q4: What is the duration of the initial contract for Engineering Services?**  
A4: The initial contract term is three (3) years, with an option to extend for two (2) additional one-year periods.  
**Text Snippet:** "[A] three (3) year, non-exclusive contract [...] with the option to extend the contract for two (2) additional one (1) year periods, cancelable by the County on sixty (60) days notice."  

**Q5: What types of engineering services are explicitly required under the "Basic Services" section?**  
A5: Services include civil, electrical, environmental, mechanical, and structural engineering; surveying; groundwater/gas monitoring; and compliance with regulatory standards.  
**Text Snippet:** "The selected firm(s) shall perform Professional Engineering Services [...] including [...] civil, electrical, environmental, mechanical and structural Engineering Services; surveying [...] groundwater and landfill gas extraction systems [...] in accordance with [...] laws, ordinances, and requirements of all governmental agencies."  

**Q6: Can the County award contracts to multiple firms under this RFP?**  
A6: Yes, the County reserves the right to award contracts to more than one qualified offeror based on expertise and project needs.  
**Text Snippet:** "The County reserves the right to award contracts to more than one qualified offeror, depending on the expertise of the offering firms and the needs of the County."
