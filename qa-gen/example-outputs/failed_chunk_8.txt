**Q1:** What conditions allow the County to terminate the contract for default?  
**A1:** The County may terminate the contract for default if the Contractor fails to deliver equipment/services, repeatedly fails to respond to maintenance requests on time, or breaches other terms after a "Show Cause Notice" and fails to cure within 10 days.  
**Text Snippet:** "The County shall have the unilateral right to terminate this contract for default... (a) the Contractor shall fail to deliver the equipment or services... (b) the Contractor shall repeatedly fail to respond to request for maintenance... (c) the Contractor shall breach any of the other cures after receiving a 'Show Cause Notice'... providing the Contractor ten (10) days to cure."  

**Q2:** What financial obligations does the Contractor have if the County terminates the contract for convenience?  
**A2:** The Contractor is paid for completed services and lease buyouts up to the termination date but cannot claim additional fees or lost profits.  
**Text Snippet:** "If the contract is terminated... for the convenience of the County, the Contractor shall be paid for the service done and the remaining buyout of the lease to the date of termination, but shall not be paid any other fees or lost profits."  

**Q3:** What are the requirements for a valid waiver of contract terms?  
**A3:** A waiver must be in writing and signed by the party granting it to be effective.  
**Text Snippet:** "Any waiver of a term of this Contract... must be in writing and signed by the party granting the waiver to be effective."  

**Q4:** Who is responsible for repairing work site damages caused by the Contractor?  
**A4:** The Contractor must repair damages at their own expense to the County’s satisfaction.  
**Text Snippet:** "Any damages... resulting from the performance of this contract shall be repaired to the satisfaction of the County at the Contractor's expense."
