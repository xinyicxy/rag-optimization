**Q1:** What are the ownership and control requirements for a service-disabled veteran-owned business entity?  
**A1:** At least 51% of the equity ownership interest must be held by service-disabled veterans, and management/daily operations must be controlled by service-disabled veterans.  
**Text Snippet:** "at least 51 percent of the equity ownership interest... is owned by one or more individuals who are service disabled veterans and both the management and daily business operations are controlled by one or more individuals who are service disabled veterans."  

**Q2:** What three criteria define a "service-disabled veteran"?  
**A2:** The veteran must have (1) served on active duty in the U.S. military, (2) been discharged honorably, and (3) received a service-connected disability rating from the VA.  
**Text Snippet:** "(i) served on active duty in the United States military ground, naval, or air service, (ii) was discharged or released under conditions other than dishonorable, and (iii) has a service-connected disability rating fixed by the United States Department of Veterans Affairs."  

**Q3:** What SCC registration options are available to bidders/offerors?  
**A3:** Options include providing an SCC ID, not being a registered entity type, being an out-of-state entity without regular Virginia operations, or submitting a legal opinion on Virginia business transactions.  
**Text Snippet:** "The Bidder or Offeror: is a corporation or other business entity with the following SCC identification number... -OR- is not a corporation, limited liability company... -OR- is an out-of-state business entity that does not regularly and continuously maintain... employees, agents, offices... in Virginia... -OR- is an out-of-state business entity that is including... an opinion of legal counsel."
